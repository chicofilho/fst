Gold Coast Human Picks

These picks may change. Stay on!!

Tier A

The Tier A choices are easy to sustain. Adriano De Souza won last years world champion and for this reason mathematics will be on his favour, I will wait one event to see how relaxed he is. Mick Fanning has had a tough year last season, I'm still in doubt if he will compete this year. For those reasons my picks will be Medina and Toledo. Medina scores well in all ranks, is the first in Momentum Rank and third in Performance Rank. Filipe Toledo has proved to be one of the best surfers in small conditions besides is the fourth Tier A choice by mathematics.

Gabriel Medina

Filipe Toledo

Tier B

I will definitely stay with this Tier B. Experience plays a huge role in Gold Coast, Taj, Slater and Joel have the mathematics in their favour. Wiggolly has only surfed once in this event, but has proved to manufacture solid scores. If you have a doubt amongst those surfers Wiggolly can be the one to change. If I decide to change him, it will be by John John or Jordy Smith. Both are top 10 surfers, Jordy is ranked low because of his last years momentum (that is why I would wait one event to start choosing him), but he has been in Round 5 several times in past Gold Coast events. John John is ranked low for having small appearance in Round 5 on past Gold Coast events (once in 2013), however, he punctuates well on Performance Rank (he is just missing a detail to do well)

Taj Burrow

Kelly Slater

Tier C

Miguel Pupo is the best Tier C choice and we have enough data to prove that. The other choice for calculation was not difficult, Kolohe Andino finished last year with some Momentum and plays not bad in Performance Rank compared to other Ticer C data. If you decide to change one of them, Sebastian Zietz is the most conservative choice, he surfs well enough to be on Tier B. The most aggressive choice would be Jack Freestone, surfed last year on Gold Coast and is eager to do well this year.
